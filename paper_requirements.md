
# Group Paper Requirements

# DATA 614: Advanced Data Analytics – Winter 2026

Timeline: Proposal (topic/idea) due Mar 17th. Draft due March 31st. Final
submission due on April 14th at noon. Presentation on April 14th.
Students are required to submit group papers on a data analytics topic. Most appropriate topics
can be business applications of one or more data analytics models/tools of your choice. The
topics should go beyond what we do in class. You can also focus on specific business problems
or cases and look into the different data analytics tools that are appropriate for that problem.
You do not need to cover everything relevant to a problem or tool, but provide a high level
introduction. I suggest papers 2-4 pages long (excluding pictures, references, etc.), including an
intro on the problem/tools and business applications and examples. You can also provide
demos of the applications and tools. These papers will be in the format of a short “white paper”
or “industry report”, while following proper academic writing guidelines, such as proper use of
references. You are required to present your papers in short format (max 10 minutes,
preferably 5 minutes) to the class on the last day of class.

# Deliverables

Each group will submit the following:
- A written report. Please limit this to 2-4 single-spaced letter size pages (excluding pictures,
references, etc.). You do not need to fill all pages to have a good paper. In fact, if your paper
is unnecessarily long, it will reduce the clarity (one of the criteria for grading).
- Presentation file. You will present your paper at the last class. The presentations are limited
to 5-10 minutes.
Please follow this framework and guidelines for your report and presentation:
Context and Problem/Opportunity
- Provide an introduction to the industry and its problem(s) and opportunity(ies). Think about
questions such as why the problem or opportunity is important. Alternatively, you can focus
on a specific data analytics tool, and introduce its applications and success stories.
- It should be clear what you are trying to accomplish in this paper, preferably within a few
sentences.
- Make sure anyone without any previous knowledge on the topic can read and understand
what you are saying.
Data Analytics Tools and Approaches
- Depending on the topic, you should provide an introduction to how the data analytic(s)
tools work, especially in the context of the business problem or opportunity, and explain its
applications. Think about how the data analytics tool(s) can help solve problems.
- [Optional] It helps to use examples, or even do a demo of the data analytics tools using a
small dataset.

# Discussion
- You should briefly discuss the implications of the tools or applications and how they can
benefit organizations.
- Discuss challenges, shortcomings, and opportunities for future efforts relevant to your
paper.
The grade distribution is as follows: Written report: 70%. Presentation: 20%. Peer evaluation:
10%.
  
# Criteria for grading
- Clarity: it is important that the report and your presentation is easy to understand, including
the problem, analysis, findings, and discussion.
- Importance and usefulness: the paper should address an important and useful problem,
context, and/or data analytics tool.
- Originality of the ideas in the paper are pluses, but less important than the above criteria.
The topic should be new to other students, in the sense that we do not cover it in the class.
If referring to other articles, use proper referencing. Here is one guide from University of
Calgary: https://libguides.ucalgary.ca/c.php?g=664585&p=4672589. There are other guides you
can find online. Refer to the Course Outline and Code of Student Conduct for definitions of
plagiarism and cheating
